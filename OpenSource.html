Producing and Reproducing the Culture of Open Source

The notion of "open-source software" arose in the 1990s as a more ideologically neutral term compared to the "free software" idea espoused by Richard Stallman and others. This paper does not wish to enter into the debate as to what erm is most appropriate for what is going on in what are commonly today called "open-source" projects, but instead to look at the extent to which the concrete capital goods produces by the open-source community depend upon cultural aspects of the movement.
